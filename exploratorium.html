Role + Team

In this brief internship at the Exploratorium in San Francisco, my work was mostly independent with opportunities for feedback from a few exhibit developers. There was a broader team that I met with a few times and ultimately presented to.

Challenge

The Exploratorium is planning a new museum in Brazil. One of the three themes to be in the museum is "Imagining the Future." This is very broad, so I needed to determine its meaning, topics worth exploring, and how 20 exhibits would properly achieve the theme.

Outcome

Using a systems approach, design thinking, and process they are familiar with, I gave exhibit developers explicit goals and steps to work toward a museum that gives every Brazilian has confidence in their ability to change the future.

Empathy

Understanding audience and stakeholders

Define

Stakeholders' needs

  • Distinct from other collections: Phenomena of the world, Learning by doing
  • Incorporate social science
  • Leverage data, analysis
  • Visitors should draw their own conclusions and visualize the future
  • Perhaps spark convos of how people can affect the future now
  • Engaging enough to bring people back

Define

New vision for the theme

Original vision

Our vision is a world in which Brazilians approach the future with a sense of possibility and optimism because they have confidence in their ability to learn and apply scientific concepts, to collaborate across boundaries, and to think creatively.

Revised Vision

Our vision is a world in which every Brazilian has confidence in their ability to change the future.

  • This makes it more timeless than “what the future will be like”
  • Compatible with visitors setting personal goals for their futures
  • Emphasize value of conversations
  • Give opportunities for debate

Define

Identify causes

Why are people not confident in the first place?

  • They haven’t had the chance to try to make a difference
  • Broken school system
  • They are “just one person”
  • They are not educated as to how
  • They feel others are ‘smarter’
  • Mental illness

Research on the floor and online

To learn about exhibits and start determining what works best, I watched visitors at the Exploratorium interact with exhibits, as well as read reports on exhibits at the Explo and at other museums around the world. This allowed me to determine what factors would work best for our exhibits in the new museum.

Handoff

Exhibit developers to now follow process

I left the team overall design recommendations, as well as a simple 6-step developer process for each exhibit that ensure designers have freedom for creativity and produce work that will help achieve the overall thematic goal. I additionally left the manager a process for deciding the final 20 exhibits.

I determined metrics and the best method of evaluation for the museum, as well as conducted a pre mortem. I specified next steps, furter research questions and recommendatoins.
